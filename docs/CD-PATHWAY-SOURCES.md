## cd-ch15 — "Delivery: PIP, borders & post-op"  *(audited + rebuilt to Swade order)*

### intro
- ✅ "At delivery you seat and finish the processed dentures … educate the patient last." — **Swade p.92** (denture delivery: steps, overall order) + **Delivery lec** "IV. Denture placement sequences." *Order follows Swade (palatal seal/borders before PIP); the lecture orders base→borders→remount→patient-ed.*

### Materials checklist
- ✅ "From sterilization: exam kit, lab handpiece + lab burs, patient goggles." — **Swade p.92** "from sterilization: exam kit, lab handpiece, lab burs, patient goggles, blood glucose kit"
- ✅ "From the lab return: the finished, polished maxillary + mandibular dentures." — **Swade p.92** "dentures from the lab" + p.93 step 8 "polish denture"
- ✅ "In clinic: PIP paste + brush + PIP remover, Mizzy spray, disclosing wax, Thompson stick, horseshoe articulating paper, denture cleanser sample, post-op written instructions, denture brush, denture storage box." — **Swade p.92** "in clinic: horseshoe articulating paper, PIP, PIP remover, Thompson stick, disclosing wax"; Mizzy = p.92 step 5; cleanser/brush/box = p.93 note

### Before the appointment
- ⚠ "Patient leaves old dentures out for 24 hours before delivery — lets tissues rebound…" — **not in Swade or the delivery lectures.** Common prosth practice; unsourced here.
- ✅/ⓘ "Confirm the lab returned the finished, polished dentures … (No casts or jigs — master casts destroyed during processing.)" — master-cast destruction = **Processing lec** (deflasking) + clinical reality (classmate); *needs exact processing-lecture line.*
- ✅/⚠ "Print post-op instructions in the patient's preferred language." — "post-op instructions printed" = **Swade p.93**; ⚠ "preferred language" not in any source.
- ⚠ "Schedule 1.5 hours." — **not in any source.**

### Step 1 — Check fit & esthetics
- ✅ "Before seating, run a finger over the intaglio and borders and smooth any sharp or rough spots." — **Swade p.92 step 2** "check denture for any sharp/rough spots & adjust"
- ✅ "Seat both dentures and ask the patient about any immediate areas of concern — pain, sharp, tight." — **Swade p.92 step 2** "ask pt if they notice any immediate areas of concern (pain, sharp, tight)"
- ✅ "Hand the patient a mirror and get their approval of the esthetics. Be positive and encouraging." — **Swade p.92 step 2** "give pt hand mirror & ask if they approve of esthetics (be encouraging & positive)"

### Step 2 — Posterior palatal seal
- ✅ "With the dentures out, have the patient say \"Ah\" and mark the vibrating line with a wet Thompson stick." — **Swade p.92 step 3** (verbatim)
- ✅ "Seat the maxillary denture and check how far its posterior border extends past the vibrating line." — **Swade p.92 step 3** "determine the extension past the vibrating line"
- ✅ "Reduce any overextension with a bur, then bevel it to a gradual feather edge toward the posterior border." — **Swade p.92 step 3** "reduce any palatal overextension … bevel … gradual slope … (feather edge)"

### Step 3 — Adjust the borders
- ✅ "Border-extension check: gently pull the cheeks and have the patient lift the tongue — the denture shouldn't unseat — then run a finger along the border…" — **Swade p.92 step 4** (verbatim)
- ✅ "To find overextended borders, use disclosing wax: roll into thin ropes, mold along the borders (≤2 mm), seat through border-molding movements … look for show-through … reduce with a lab bur, repeat." — **Swade p.94–95** adjustment step 5 (disclosing-wax procedure)
- ⓘ "Smooth and polish adjusted borders." — implied by Swade polish step (p.93 step 8).

### Step 4 — Fit the intaglio (PIP)
- ⚠ "Start with the maxillary (more retentive, easier to evaluate)." — arch order **not specified in Swade**; reasonable but unsourced.
- ✅ "Dry the intaglio." — **Swade p.92 step 5** "thoroughly dry the denture"
- ✅ "Apply a thin, uniform layer of PIP … brushing all in one direction so show-through is easy to read." — **Swade p.92 step 5** (verbatim)
- ✅ "Spray Mizzy on the residual ridge so the paste doesn't stick to the mucosa (don't let the mouth go dry)." — **Swade p.92 step 5** (verbatim)
- ✅ "Seat with light, even hand pressure on the first molars — do NOT have the patient bite. ~5 seconds." — **Swade p.92 step 5** "do not apply firm pressure! do not have the patient bite! only … light hand pressure on 1st molars … ~5 seconds"
- ✅ "Remove and inspect. Show-through — where the PIP is pushed away to nothing — marks a pressure point." — **Swade p.92 step 5** "examine … for areas of show-through (high areas)"
- ✅ "Reduce those spots conservatively with a lab bur. Re-apply PIP and repeat until the layer is uniform and undisturbed." — **Swade p.92 step 5** "use lab burs to reduce high spots … repeat … until denture is ideal"
- ⓘ "Repeat for the mandibular." — Swade applies PIP to both dentures.

### Step 5 — Check occlusion
- ✅ "Mark occlusion, lateral movements, and protrusion with horseshoe articulating paper." — **Swade p.93 step 6** (verbatim)
- ✅ "Reduce denture teeth that hit too high with a lab bur; re-evaluate and repeat. Easiest to pick one arch and adjust only that arch." — **Swade p.93 step 6** (verbatim)
- ✅ "Goals: even contact on both sides on multiple/all posterior teeth; in protrusion, a posterior contact on each side plus anterior contact." — **Swade p.93 step 6** goals (verbatim)
- ✅ "Keep it light at delivery — the patient still has to adapt, and the intaglio will need more adjustment at the 24-hour follow-up." — **Swade p.95** adjustment step 8 "usually at the 24 hour followup, you don't adjust occlusion too much…"

### Step 6 — Polish
- ✅ "Polish the outside of the dentures with a rag wheel + pumice in the support lab. Never polish the intaglio — it changes the fit." — **Swade p.93 step 8** "polish … rag wheel + pumice in the support lab … only polish the outside; do not polish the intaglio"

### Step 7 — Patient education (POI)
- ✅ "First night: keep the dentures in until the 24-hour follow-up … After that, remove them at night and keep them in water." — **Swade p.93** "instructed not to remove dentures until 24 hr followup" + **Delivery lec p.603** "Remove dentures at night and keep them in water" + **Swade p.184** "soak overnight in water"
- ✅ "Eat soft foods and small bites to start." — **Delivery lec p.591** "Eat: Relatively soft foods, small bites"
- ✅ "Speak: practice by reading aloud in front of a mirror." — **Delivery lec p.592** "Speak: Practice reading aloud, in front of mirror"
- ✅ "Saliva: expect a temporary increase in flow at first — it settles." — **Delivery lec p.593** "Saliva: Temporary increase flow"
- ✅ "Soreness is an expected tissue response; sore spots stay tender for a few days even after we adjust them." — **Delivery lec p.595** "Soreness: Expected tissue response" + **Swade p.96** "sore spots will still feel tender … take a few days to heal"
- ✅ "Wash the dentures after eating." — **Delivery lec p.600** + **Swade p.184** "rinse … after eating"
- ✅ "Clean with a denture brush + denture toothpaste … massage or brush the gums for stimulation." — **Delivery lec p.601–602** "Denture brush/denture toothpaste" + "Massage or brush tissue for stimulation" + **Swade p.184**
- ✅ "Soak in water when out (or Polident + water). Never hot water — it warps the acrylic." — **Swade p.184** "soak … in water (or Polident + water); only warm or cool water — never hot water"
- ✅ "If the denture feels loose, a small amount of denture adhesive (Polident / Polygrip) is fine." — **Swade p.93** note "Gave pt … polident, polygrip"
- ✅ "Bring the dentures to each visit for ultrasonic cleaning." — **Swade p.184** "bring dentures/partials … for dentist to clean in ultrasonic"
- ✅ "Next visit: 24-hour follow-up (sooner if a severe adjustment was needed; ~3 days if minor)." — **Swade p.93** "NV: 24 hr post-delivery followup" + **Delivery lec p.605–606** "1 Day- severe adjustment; 3 day- minor adjustment"

### Issues at delivery (callout)
- ⓘ "Denture won't seat: reapply PIP; identify an undercut intaglio area or an overextended border." — inference from Swade PIP (intaglio) + disclosing-wax (border) steps.
- ⚠ "Patient says \"bite feels off\": re-verify both bases are fully seated first…" — inference; not explicit in a source.
- ⓘ "Patient gags on insertion: posterior border too thick or overextended…" — relates to Swade palatal-seal step 3; not stated as a gag remedy.
- ✅ "Sore spot on insertion: mark with the Thompson stick BEFORE removing; transfer to the intaglio; PIP-adjust." — **Swade p.94** adjustment step 3 (Thompson-stick sore-spot procedure)
- ⚠ "Patient very anxious / overwhelmed: reassure that adjustment over the first 2–4 weeks is standard…" — counseling; not in any source.

## cd-ch2 — "Custom tray fabrication"  *(audited — strongly sourced)*

Primary source: **Custom Tray lecture** (`Custom tray_Boxing_ Technique.pdf`, Tan/Touloumi) + **Custom Tray Grading Sheet_2022**. Relief landmarks: Edentulous Anatomy lec.

- ✅ Materials (diagnostic cast, red/blue pencil, baseplate wax, Triad, Vaseline, #7 spatula, #11 scalpel, acrylic burs) — lecture bench list (lines 6–29).
- ✅ Step 1 mark cast: red = vestibule depth, **blue = 2 mm inside red** for border-mold space, **posterior = RED only**, red = relief areas — lecture #1–2 (lines 34–39). Specific relief sites (mid-palatal, incisive papilla, tori, mylohyoid, knife-edge) = Edentulous Anatomy lec.
- ✅ Step 2 prep: vaseline cast → drip-wax block-out → **vaseline over block-out** — lecture #3–5 (41–48).
- ✅ Step 3 spacer: warm baseplate wax "not dripping," cut to blue line, **do NOT vaseline after (spacer stays in tray for border molding)** — lecture #6–7 (49–59).
- ✅ Step 4 Triad: one sheet, no trapped air, trim to red line — lecture #8 (61–63).
- ✅ Step 5 handle/finger rests: handle clears lip; **mand finger rests ≤ 2/3 retromolar pad height**; build from offcuts — lecture #9 + "Make sure" (65–73).
- ✅ Step 6 cure: **~1 min, not less**; cool before removing; re-cure off the cast — lecture #10–11 (76–80).
- ✅ Step 7 finish + callout grading: even single-layer thickness, spacer retained, frenal/muscle clearance, smooth borders — lecture (82–88) + grading sheet.
- **✂ CUT (fabricated precision, not in any source):** "~120 °F" wax-bath temp · "warm the Triad / some come pre-warmed" (Triad is light-cured, not thermoplastic) · "~10 mm" handle height · "rotating table at 400–500 nm" · "~5 min" cooling time.
